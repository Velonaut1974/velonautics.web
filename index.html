The Triple-Layer Architecture

A modular framework for regulatory isolation and value attribution.

The foundation for legal operation. Layer I automates the monitoring, reporting, and verification (MRV) requirements for both mass-based (ETS) and intensity-based (FuelEU) regimes. By forecasting surrender requirements and managing pooling accounts, it provides a predictive safety net to mitigate price volatility of EUAs and avoid non-compliance penalties.
The heart of VELONAUTICS. Layer II implements a strict digital isolation protocol through Joule-tagging.
"It guarantees that one Joule of green energy is never sold twice."
By isolating mandatory compliance from voluntary value, we prevent double-counting and ensure that any environmental attribute utilized in Layer I is irreversibly locked and cannot be claimed as additionality in Layer III.
The engine for voluntary value. Layer III identifies and aggregates "Additionality"—the emissions reductions achieved beyond regulatory mandates. These surpluses are structured into high-integrity Scope 3 Insetting assets. This enables ship operators to monetize their decarbonization investments by selling verified performance directly to cargo owners.

The Regulatory Collision

Navigating the asynchronies between mass-based carbon pricing and energy intensity targets requires a dedicated decision infrastructure.

EU-ETS (Mass-Based)

Metric: Absolute CO2 Emissions

The EU Emissions Trading System operates on a 'Cap & Trade' principle. Ship operators must surrender one EUA (European Union Allowance) for every ton of verified CO2. The primary goal is volume reduction through financial incentives, where cost is directly proportional to the total mass of fuel consumed.

FuelEU Maritime (Intensity)

Metric: GHG Intensity (gCO2e/MJ)

FuelEU focuses on the energy quality. It sets limits on the greenhouse gas intensity of energy used on board, rewarding renewable fuels through pooling and banking. Non-compliance results in penalties based on the 'compliance balance' deficit, making strategic fuel mix orchestration essential.
